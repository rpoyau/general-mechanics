\subsection{Thermodynamics to Newtonian rate laws (constant‑free)}
\label{corollary:thermo-newton}

\paragraph{Setup.}
On configuration–time space \((q,t)\) \axref{axioms:path-rate},
assume a quadratic kinetic term with a positive‑definite, window‑constant mass metric \(M\) and an external potential:
\begin{equation}
H(q,P,t)=\tfrac12\,P\!\cdot M^{-1}P \;+\; U(q,t),\qquad M>0 .
\label{corollary:thermo-newton:eq:kinetic}
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Derivation.}
From the canonical rate equations \eqref{axioms:path-rate:eq:canonical} applied to
\eqref{corollary:thermo-newton:eq:kinetic}, \(\dot q=M^{-1}P\), \(\dot P=-\partial_q U\).
With \(\mathbf p:=M\dot q\) and \(\mathbf f:=-\,\partial_q U\), one obtains the Newtonian rate law
\begin{equation}
\dot{\mathbf p} \;=\; \mathbf f.
\label{corollary:thermo-newton:eq:primary}
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Specialization.}
Work–power identity for \(K(P):=\tfrac12\,P\!\cdot M^{-1}P=\tfrac12\,\dot q\!\cdot M\dot q\):
\begin{equation}
\frac{dK}{dt}
= \dot q\!\cdot \dot P
= \dot q\!\cdot \mathbf f
= \mathbf f\!\cdot \mathbf v ,
\qquad \mathbf v:=\dot q .
\label{corollary:thermo-newton:eq:power}
\end{equation}
If \(U\) is time‑independent, \(\dot H=0\) for \(H=K+U\).

\paragraph{Calibration.}
With the one‑datum linear sector mapping \eqref{app:kick:eq:calib-map} and scale \eqref{app:kick:eq:one-datum},
measured sector fields satisfy
\begin{equation}
\mathbf g=c_M\,\mathbf E_I,
\qquad
\mathbf E=c_Q\,\mathbf E_I .
\label{corollary:thermo-newton:eq:calib}
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Falsification.}
In a constant‑mass, time‑independent \(U\) regime, if measured \(\mathbf p(t)\) and \(\mathbf f(t)\) on the same window fail
\(\dot{\mathbf p}=\mathbf f\) by more than the declared estimation bounds, either the Hamiltonian choice
\eqref{corollary:thermo-newton:eq:kinetic} or the calibration \eqref{corollary:thermo-newton:eq:calib} is inconsistent with the axioms invoked.
