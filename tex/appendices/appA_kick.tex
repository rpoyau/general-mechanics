\section{Kick (Kz) units \& Kram anchors}
\label{app:kick}

This appendix fixes the unit convention used throughout: write energy‑like quantities as \emph{rates} by dividing SI units by $h$. This keeps every balance and bound in rate form and avoids bare constants in the spine of the framework. Cross‑references to screen geometry use App.~\ref{app:nD-screens}; noise kernels and covariance propagation are in App.~\ref{app:noise-kernel}; one‑datum sector calibrations are collected in App.~\ref{app:calibrations}.

% ---------------------------- A.1 --------------------------------------
\subsection{Kz (Kick) convention}
\label{appA:kz-conv}

We define Kz (\emph{Kick}) units by dividing by Planck's constant $h$:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:appA-kz-map}
E^{(\mathrm{Kz})}=\frac{E}{h},\qquad
M^{(\mathrm{Kz})}=\frac{mc^{2}}{h},\qquad
T^{(\mathrm{Kz})}=\frac{k_{B}T}{h},\qquad
P^{(\mathrm{Kz})}=\frac{P_{\rm SI}}{h}.
\end{equation}
Thus $E^{(\mathrm{Kz})}$, $M^{(\mathrm{Kz})}$, $T^{(\mathrm{Kz})}$, and $P^{(\mathrm{Kz})}$ all carry units of s$^{-1}$. In these units, the \emph{erasure power divided by temperature} is a \emph{rate}, which is the form used in the time--information bounds (Corollary~\ref{corollary:time-info}).

\paragraph{Sanity check.}
In Kz, the Landauer/erasure rate bound for reliable, durable records reads (bits/s)
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:appA-erasure-rate}
\frac{\Delta I_{\rm bits}}{\Delta t}\ \le\ \frac{P^{(\mathrm{Kz})}}{T^{(\mathrm{Kz})}\,\ln 2},
\end{equation}
and the same expression without $\ln 2$ holds in nats/s. See the rate and time forms in Corollary~\ref{corollary:time-info}.

% ---------------------------- A.2 --------------------------------------
\subsection{Bits, nats, and entropic flux}
\label{appA:bits-nats}

If boundary counts are recorded in bits,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:appA-bits-nats}
j_{\text{nats}}=(\ln 2)\,j_{\text{bits}},\qquad
S_{\text{nats}}=(\ln 2)\,S_{\text{bits}}.
\end{equation}
All screen/tube relations (e.g.\ inverse‑area scaling on screens from C0--C1) and bounds are invariant under this change of unit; only numerical prefactors in calibrations change by $\ln 2$.

% ---------------------------- A.3 --------------------------------------
\subsection{Kram anchors (optional decimal ladder)}
\label{appA:kram}

When rough magnitudes span many decades, we optionally name logarithmic anchors for Kz rates:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:appA-kram}
1\,k=10^{47}\,\mathrm{Kz},\qquad
1\,kk=10^{50}\,\mathrm{Kz},\qquad
1\,Mk=10^{53}\,\mathrm{Kz},\qquad
1\,Gk=10^{56}\,\mathrm{Kz},\qquad
1\,Tk=10^{59}\,\mathrm{Kz}.
\end{equation}

% ---------------------------- A.4 --------------------------------------
\subsection{One--datum sector calibrations (static readouts)}
\label{appA:calibrations}\label{axiom:calibrations} % alias for legacy refs

The informational field is $\mathbf E_I:=-\nabla\Phi_I$, with static closure $\mathbf j=-\kappa\,\mathbf E_I$ (Axiom~D2, eq.~\eqref{eq:static-closure}). Measured sectors are defined by a \emph{single} linear calibration (Kz once):
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:appA-calib-map}
\mathbf g=c_M\,\mathbf E_I,\qquad
\mathbf E=c_Q\,\mathbf E_I,\qquad
\mathbf j_{\rm th}=\kappa_T\,\mathbf E_I .
\end{equation}
Each constant $(c_M,c_Q,\kappa_T)$ is fixed by one datum in that sector (see App.~\ref{app:calibrations}).

\paragraph{How one datum fixes the scale.}
On a time‑frozen, source‑free annulus, the directed flux $\Phi$ through a radius‑$r$ screen is constant and
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:appA-screen-law}
\big\langle j_n\big\rangle(r)=\frac{\Phi}{A_n(r)},\qquad
\big|\mathbf E_I(r)\big|=\frac{1}{\kappa}\,\frac{\Phi}{A_n(r)},\qquad
A_n(r)=S_{n-1}\,r^{\,n-1}\ \ \text{(App.~\ref{app:nD-screens}, eq.~\eqref{appC:eq:areas})}.
\end{equation}
Given a reference screen $r_\star$ around a source with flux $\Phi_\star$ and a measured field $Y_\star\in\{g_\star,E_\star,j_{{\rm th},\star}\}$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:appA-one-datum}
c_{\rm sec}
=\frac{Y_\star}{|\mathbf E_I(r_\star)|}
=Y_\star\,\frac{\kappa\,A_n(r_\star)}{\Phi_\star},
\qquad \text{(sec}\in\{M,Q,{\rm th}\}).
\end{equation}
No bare constants enter beyond this datum.

\paragraph{Ratio predictions (constant‑free).}
Once $c_{\rm sec}$ is fixed,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:appA-ratio}
Y(r)=c_{\rm sec}\,|\mathbf E_I(r)|
=Y_\star\,\frac{\Phi(r)}{\Phi_\star}\,\frac{A_n(r_\star)}{A_n(r)}.
\end{equation}
In lossless statics $\Phi(r)=\Phi_\star$, so $Y(r)=Y_\star\,(r_\star/r)^{\,n-1}$ (cf.\ C1).

% ---------------------------- A.5 --------------------------------------
\subsection{Capacity and erasure in Kz (operational knobs)}
\label{appA:capacity-erasure}

When a channel class and budgets are specified, the reliable capacity (bits/s) is a functional
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:appA-capacity}
C_{\rm chan}^{(\text{bits})}(\varepsilon)\ =\ \mathcal C\big[R^{(a)};\,\mathcal N,\,\mathcal B;\,\varepsilon\big],
\end{equation}
where $R^{(a)}$ are boundary controls (apertures, alphabets), $\mathcal N$ is the noise kernel (see App.~\ref{app:noise-kernel}), and $\mathcal B$ collects budgets (power $P^{(\mathrm{Kz})}$, temperature $T^{(\mathrm{Kz})}$, area $A$, time window $\Delta t$, reliability $\varepsilon$). The time--information bounds used in the body (Corollary~\ref{corollary:time-info}) combine three independent ceilings:
\[
\frac{\Delta I_{\text{bits}}}{\Delta t}
\ \le\
\min\!\left\{
\frac{\overline{J}_{A}}{\ln 2},\quad
C_{\rm chan}^{(\text{bits})}(\varepsilon),\quad
\frac{P^{(\mathrm{Kz})}}{T^{(\mathrm{Kz})}\,\ln 2}
\right\},
\]
with the last term originating from \eqref{eq:appA-erasure-rate} when durable records are formed and later erased.

% ---------------------------- A.6 --------------------------------------
\subsection{Quick conversions and notes}
\label{appA:conversions}

For any SI quantity $X$, $X^{(\mathrm{Kz})}=X/h$.
Examples (units shown as s$^{-1}$):
\[
E\,[\mathrm{J}]\mapsto E/h,\qquad
E\,[\mathrm{eV}]\mapsto \frac{E\,[\mathrm{eV}]\cdot e}{h},\qquad
T\,[\mathrm{K}]\mapsto \frac{k_B T}{h},\qquad
P\,[\mathrm{W}]\mapsto \frac{P}{h}.
\]
All predictions in the main text are expressed in Kz; numerical evaluations (if any) convert back to SI at the final step.

\medskip
\noindent\emph{Summary.}
Kz turns energies, masses, temperatures, and powers into rates, aligning all balances and bounds with the informational viewpoint. One‑datum, sector‑specific calibrations then translate the informational field into measured readouts without introducing bare constants; capacity and erasure appear as independent, operational ceilings in the same units.
