\section{Screens in $n$ dimensions: areas, harmonics, Green functions}
\label{app:nD-screens}

This appendix collects geometric identities for $n$-dimensional screens and
their consequences for static amplitudes under the balance law (D1; eq.~\eqref{eq:stokes-identity})
and the static closure (D2; eq.~\eqref{eq:static-closure}). All amplitudes are informational;
measured sectors follow by one--datum calibration (Appendix~\ref{app:calibrations}).

% ----------------------------- C.1 -------------------------------------
\subsection{Sphere areas and ball volumes}
\label{appC:areas}

Let $S_{n-1}$ denote the area of the unit $(n{-}1)$--sphere in $\mathbb R^{n}$ and
$B_n(r)$ the ball of radius $r$. Then
\begin{equation}
S_{n-1}=\frac{2\,\pi^{n/2}}{\Gamma(n/2)}, 
\qquad
A_n(r)=S_{n-1}\,r^{\,n-1},
\qquad
V_n(r)=\frac{\pi^{n/2}}{\Gamma(\tfrac{n}{2}+1)}\,r^{\,n}.
\label{appC:eq:areas}
\end{equation}
Checks: $S_0=2$, $S_1=2\pi$, $S_2=4\pi$ so that
$A_1(r)=2$, $A_2(r)=2\pi r$, $A_3(r)=4\pi r^2$.

% ----------------------------- C.2 -------------------------------------
\subsection{Radial harmonics and the static screen law}
\label{appC:radial-harmonic}

On a time-frozen slice with $\sigma=0$ in an annulus and with the static
closure $\mathbf j=\kappa\nabla\Phi_I$ and $\mathbf E_I:=-\nabla\Phi_I$
(D2; eq.~\eqref{eq:static-closure}), the local balance (D1) reads
$\nabla\!\cdot\mathbf j=0$, hence $\Delta\Phi_I=0$ away from sources.
For radial $\Phi_I(r)$,
\begin{equation}
\frac{d}{dr}\!\left(r^{\,n-1}\,\frac{d\Phi_I}{dr}\right)=0
\ \Longrightarrow\
\frac{d\Phi_I}{dr}=\frac{C}{r^{\,n-1}},
\qquad
|\mathbf E_I(r)|=\frac{|C|}{r^{\,n-1}} .
\label{appC:eq:radial-harmonic}
\end{equation}
Let $\Phi(r):=\int_{\Sigma(r)}\mathbf j\!\cdot n\,dA$ be the directed flux
through a spherical screen $\Sigma(r)$. With $\mathbf j=-\kappa\,\mathbf E_I$,
\begin{equation}
\Phi(r)=-\kappa\,|\mathbf E_I(r)|\,A_n(r)
\ \Rightarrow\
|\mathbf E_I(r)|=\frac{1}{\kappa}\,\frac{|\Phi|}{A_n(r)}
=\frac{|\Phi|}{\kappa\,S_{n-1}}\,\frac{1}{r^{\,n-1}} .
\label{appC:eq:screen-law}
\end{equation}
Equation \eqref{appC:eq:screen-law} is the full-screen version of the cone/tube
rule used in the main text (C0--C1; see \S\ref{corollary:info-gas} and \S\ref{corollary:static-screen}).

% ----------------------------- C.3 -------------------------------------
\subsection{Green functions of the Laplacian (normalization by $S_{n-1}$)}
\label{appC:greens}

Let $G_n(x)$ solve $\Delta G_n=\delta$ in $\mathbb R^n$ with the
normalization compatible with \eqref{appC:eq:screen-law}:
\begin{equation}
G_n(x)=
\begin{cases}
-\dfrac{1}{2}\,|x|, & n=1,\\[6pt]
\dfrac{1}{2\pi}\,\ln |x|, & n=2,\\[8pt]
-\dfrac{1}{(n-2)\,S_{n-1}}\,|x|^{\,2-n}, & n\ge 3.
\end{cases}
\label{appC:eq:greens}
\end{equation}
Then $\Phi_I=G_n * \rho$ solves $\Delta\Phi_I=\rho$ and
$\mathbf E_I=-\nabla\Phi_I$. The factors of $S_{n-1}$ ensure that integrating
$\nabla\!\cdot\mathbf j=0$ on a ball and using $A_n(r)$ reproduces the
$1/r^{\,n-1}$ amplitude.

% ----------------------------- C.4 -------------------------------------
\subsection{Sector (cone) and tube forms}
\label{appC:sector}

For a conical sector of fixed solid angle $\Delta\Omega$,
\begin{equation}
A_\Delta(r)=\Delta\Omega\,r^{\,n-1},\qquad
\big\langle j_n\big\rangle(r)=\frac{\Phi_\Delta}{A_\Delta(r)},
\qquad
|\mathbf E_I(r)|=\frac{1}{\kappa}\,\frac{\Phi_\Delta}{\Delta\Omega}\,\frac{1}{r^{\,n-1}} .
\label{appC:eq:sector}
\end{equation}
For a narrow tube whose side surface is tangent to $j$, the directed tube flux
is constant along the tube; cross--sectional averages satisfy
$\langle j_n\rangle=\Phi/A_\perp$ with fixed $A_\perp$.

% ----------------------------- C.5 -------------------------------------
\subsection{Multipoles on $S^{n-1}$ (outside bounded sources)}
\label{appC:multipole}

If $\sigma=0$ outside a bounded region, $\Phi_I$ is harmonic there and admits
the expansion
\begin{equation}
\Phi_I(r,\Omega)=\sum_{\ell=0}^{\infty} r^{-(\ell+n-2)}
\sum_{m} a_{\ell m}\,Y_{\ell m}(\Omega)\qquad (n\ge 3),
\label{appC:eq:multipole}
\end{equation}
with $Y_{\ell m}$ the spherical harmonics on $S^{n-1}$. The leading term
($\ell=0$) reproduces \eqref{appC:eq:radial-harmonic}; higher multipoles decay
faster and average to zero on full screens.

% ----------------------------- C.6 -------------------------------------
\subsection{Notes for $n=1$ and $n=2$}
\label{appC:notes-n12}

For $n=2$, harmonic potentials are logarithmic and the static field decays as
$1/r$, consistent with \eqref{appC:eq:screen-law}. For $n=1$, the screen area
is $A_1(r)=2$ (two points), so static amplitudes are $r$--independent along a
line/tube. These are the confinement limits discussed in C8 (\S\ref{corollary:dimensional}).

% ----------------------------- C.7 -------------------------------------
\subsection{Conventions and units}
\label{appC:units}

The results above are informational. Measured sector fields follow from the
one--datum calibrations $\mathbf g=c_M\mathbf E_I$,
$\mathbf E=c_Q\mathbf E_I$ (Appendix~\ref{app:calibrations}). Rates are expressed in Kz (SI$/h$).
No bare constants appear beyond the single calibration per sector.
