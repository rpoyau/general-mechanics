\section{Local-horizon (Jacobson-style) calibrated sketch}
\label{app:jacobson}

We record a constant-free derivation that converts local geometric focusing into a calibrated
stress--geometry relation used in the main text as a \emph{consistency tool}, not as an axiom.

\subsection{Setup: local wedge, generators, and kinematics}

At any event $p\in M$, choose a local Rindler wedge with future-directed generators $k^a$
(null in the reversible limit, or timelike and highly boosted in practice). Let $\lambda$ be an affine
parameter along $k^a$, $\theta:=\nabla_a k^a$ the expansion, and $A(\lambda)$ the cross-sectional
area of a thin pencil orthogonal to $k^a$. The basic identities are
\begin{equation}
\frac{d}{d\lambda}\ln A(\lambda) \;=\; \theta(\lambda),
\qquad
\frac{d\theta}{d\lambda}
\;=\; -\tfrac12\,\theta^2 - \sigma_{ab}\sigma^{ab} - R_{ab}\,k^a k^b
\quad(\omega_{ab}=0),
\label{appD:eq:ray}
\end{equation}
with shear $\sigma_{ab}$ and vanishing vorticity $\omega_{ab}$ for hypersurface-orthogonal
generators. \emph{Positive} $R_{ab}k^a k^b$ increases focusing (drives $\theta$ downward).

\subsection{Area change in the reversible limit}

Take $\theta(0)=0$ at $p$ (stationary cross-section) and neglect $\sigma_{ab}\sigma^{ab}$ in the
reversible limit. Integrating \eqref{appD:eq:ray} to first nontrivial order gives
\begin{equation}
\delta A
\;=\;
-\!\int\!\!\int R_{ab}\,k^a k^b \;\lambda\; d\lambda\, dA,
\label{appD:eq:dA}
\end{equation}
where $dA$ is the area element on the transverse slice through $p$.

\subsection{Energy (heat) flux across the local horizon}

Let $\mathcal T_{ab}^{(\mathrm{cal})}$ denote the \emph{calibrated} stress tensor of the informational
channel (same sector calibration as in statics). The energy (heat) flux through the horizon patch is
\begin{equation}
\delta Q
\;=\;
\int\!\!\int \mathcal T_{ab}^{(\mathrm{cal})}\,k^a k^b \;\lambda\; d\lambda\, dA .
\label{appD:eq:dQ}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Clausius with calibrated densities and the focusing equality}

Assign a calibrated entropy density $s_H^{(\mathrm{cal})}$ (nats per area) to the local horizon and a
calibrated temperature $T_H^{(\mathrm{cal})}$ (Kz) for the wedge. The reversible Clausius statement reads
\begin{equation}
\delta Q \;=\; T_H^{(\mathrm{cal})}\,\delta S,
\qquad
\delta S \;=\; s_H^{(\mathrm{cal})}\,\delta A.
\label{appD:eq:clausius}
\end{equation}
Combining \eqref{appD:eq:dA}--\eqref{appD:eq:clausius} and requiring equality for all patches and for all
generators $k^a$ through $p$ yields the local calibrated relation
\begin{equation}
R_{ab}\,k^a k^b
\;\overset{\mathrm{cal}}{=}\;
8\pi\,\mathcal T^{(\mathrm{cal})}_{ab}\,k^a k^b ,
\qquad
\text{(same single-sector calibration as in statics).}
\label{appD:eq:calibrated}
\end{equation}
The numerical factor is a dimensionless normalization absorbed by the single calibration (choice of
$s_H^{(\mathrm{cal})}$ and $T_H^{(\mathrm{cal})}$). Equation \eqref{appD:eq:calibrated} is precisely the
focusing consistency used in the body (see eq.~\eqref{eq:info-einstein}); no separate coupling constant is introduced.

\subsection{Remarks and scope}

\begin{itemize}\itemsep2pt
\item \textbf{Dissipation.} Away from the reversible limit, shear contributes via
$-\sigma_{ab}\sigma^{ab}$ in \eqref{appD:eq:ray}; the corresponding inequality appears as positive
production $\sigma\ge 0$ in the Stokes identity of the main text (cf.\ \eqref{eq:stokes-identity}--\eqref{eq:cut-identity} and App.~\ref{app:forms-stokes}, eqs.~\eqref{appB:eq:stokes}--\eqref{appB:eq:cut}).
\item \textbf{Calibration unity.} The \emph{same} one--datum calibration that ties static screen
amplitudes to the informational field is used here to fix $(s_H^{(\mathrm{cal})},T_H^{(\mathrm{cal})})$,
ensuring constant-free equality in \eqref{appD:eq:calibrated}.
\item \textbf{Non-claim.} Relation \eqref{appD:eq:calibrated} is employed as a \emph{consistency tool}
on the same manifold region and boundary; it is not elevated to an axiom of the framework (see Corollary~\ref{corollary:geometry} for the operational use of focusing and area evolution).
\end{itemize}

\medskip
\noindent\emph{Summary.}
Local Clausius, together with Raychaudhuri focusing, yields a constant-free, calibrated equality
between $R_{ab}k^a k^b$ and $\mathcal T^{(\mathrm{cal})}_{ab}k^a k^b$. This mirrors the static
screen laws on the same boundary and completes the geometry-as-tool perspective (see \S\ref{corollary:geometry}).

\medskip
\noindent\emph{Literature note.}
The Raychaudhuri equation \eqref{appD:eq:ray} and focusing background follow standard presentations
(Raychaudhuri~\cite{Raychaudhuri1955}; Wald~\cite{Wald1984}; Poisson~\cite{Poisson2004}). The local-horizon
Clausius equality underlying \eqref{appD:eq:calibrated} traces to Jacobson~\cite{Jacobson1995}; the Unruh
temperature for accelerated frames is \cite{Unruh1976}. These are provenance pointers only; our equality is
calibrated and used as a consistency tool on the same region.
